Global Airbnb Performance Dashboard

From early growth to COVID-19 impact- a data story across 10 global cities.


Project Overview:

This dashboard explores how Airbnb evolved as a platform from its early introduction in 2008 to the disruption caused by COVID-19 in 2020.
Rather than just presenting numbers, the goal was to tell a business story identifying turning points, market leaders, pricing gaps, and satisfaction patterns across 10 major cities worldwide.

What This Dashboard Covers:

Page 1 - Growth Story

KPI cards: total listings, hosts, cities, property types, and reviews
New listings trend across Airbnb's six lifecycle phases - Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline → Reinvention → COVID-19
Breakdown by listing type: Entire Place, Private Room, Shared Room, Hotel Room

Page 2 - City & Market Intelligence

Market share by city using a Pareto chart (Superhot vs Normal listings)
Average pricing comparison across all property types
City-wise ratings heatmap - accuracy, cleanliness, communication, location, and value


Key Insights:

Insight 2015 was the peak year for new listings globally. Growth slowed in 2016–2017 due to stricter local regulations. Airbnb turned profitable in late 2016-2017 was its first fully profitable year. A sharp drop is visible during the COVID-19 period (2020)Paris, NYC & Sydney alone account for 48% of all listings and reviews. Hotel rooms cost nearly 2x compared to shared rooms on the platform. Mexico City & Rio de Janeiro are the top-rated cities overall. Hong Kong & Istanbul score the lowest across rating dimensions, Cleanliness and value for money are the two weakest metrics globally
